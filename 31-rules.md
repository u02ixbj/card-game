# 31 — Rules & Reference

## Overview
Thirty-one (also known as Scat, Blitz, Cadillac, and Cad) is a card game where players compete to assemble a hand of three cards totalling as close to 31 as possible in a single suit.

## Players
2–8 players.

## The Deck
One standard 52-card deck (no jokers). The stock pile is never reshuffled — if it runs out, the round ends immediately and all hands are revealed.

## Goal
Have the highest-scoring hand (by single-suit total) at the end of each round. The lowest scorer(s) lose a life. Last player with lives remaining wins.

## Setup / Lives
Each player starts with 3 lives. Losing all lives eliminates you from the game. The last player standing wins.

## Card Values
- Ace = 11
- King, Queen, Jack, Ten = 10
- All other cards = face value

Only cards of the **same suit** are added together. A mixed-suit hand counts only its highest single card.

**Examples:**
- A♥ K♥ Q♥ = 31 (blitz!)
- 9♠ 8♠ 7♥ = 17 (only the two spades count)
- 5♦ 5♣ 5♥ = 5 (all different suits; best single card = 5)

## Turn Structure
Starting with the player to the dealer's left and going clockwise, on your turn you must choose one of:
1. **Draw** the top card from the stock pile, then **discard** one card from your hand, OR
2. **Take** the top card from the discard pile, then **discard** one card from your hand, OR
3. **Knock** (see below — used instead of drawing)

Your discarded card goes face-up to the top of the discard pile.

## Knocking
A player may **knock** when they believe their hand is strong enough. Knocking replaces the player's draw — no card is taken. After a knock, every other active player gets exactly one final turn (draw/take → discard). Then all hands are revealed.

The knocker can still lose a life if their hand turns out to be the lowest at reveal.

## Blitz (Exactly 31)
If after discarding, a player's three-card hand totals exactly 31 in one suit, it is a **blitz**. The round ends immediately — all other active players lose a life. The blitzer is safe.

## Special Hands
Three of a kind has no special value. Only single-suit totals count.

## Round End & Who Loses a Life
- The active player(s) with the **lowest** score lose one life.
- If multiple players tie for lowest, all of them lose a life.
- In a blitz: the blitzer is safe; all other active players lose one life.

## Stock Running Out
If the stock pile is empty when a player needs to draw, the round ends immediately. All hands are revealed and the lowest scorer(s) lose a life as normal.

## Elimination
When a player reaches 0 lives they are eliminated and sit out subsequent rounds. Remaining active players continue.

## One Tie, All Tie
If a round would eliminate all remaining active players at once (e.g. the last two players both on 1 life tie for the lowest score), no one is eliminated. Instead, **all players** — including any previously eliminated — are brought back with 3 fresh lives and play continues.

## Winning
The last player with at least one life wins.

## Rotating Dealer
The deal rotates clockwise among active players each round.
